Our Beginning

Zach and Lynde both moved to Madison around the same time. Lynde moved for a new adventure and because she knew Nate and Amanda Hobert, who moved to plant The Vine Church. Zach was still living in Platteville, WI finishing his degree in electrical engineering but came to Madison to do a nine-month internship with Extreme Engineering Solutions. They met while attending the Vine, where they belonged to the same city group (small group/Bible study). Zach and Lynde at that point were friends, but only that. Zach went back to Platteville to finish his degree and slipped from Lynde’s radar (for now). One year later, Zach was offered a full time job at Extreme and moved back to Madison; he slipped by unnoticed for a few months, but soon Lynde realized he was back in town and his social calendar became much more full! Zach and Lynde remained friends for a couple more years. Time came to plant the next church, Redeemer City. Zach and Lynde both agreed to be a part of the core/startup team. Soon they were spending 3-4 days a week together because of frisbee and church related activities. Their friendship grew as they realized they both enjoyed spending time outdoors, cooking, and then of course eating! Eventually, Zach realized he wanted to be more than just friends with Lynde, so he asked her out. Zach and Lynde dated for about 3.667 months and then he asked her to be his forever.

Our Future

We are striving (though not perfectly) to keep Christ at the center of our relationship. We recognize that we need to put our hope, significance, and joy in Christ and what he has done for us on the Cross. We ask for prayer as we continue to learn what it looks like to serve God, serve each other, and serve others before serving ourselves. Pray that we would continue to spend time in God’s Word for wisdom and guidance; that we wouldn’t look to each other for worth and happiness but to the One who has given us all we need, want, and could ever hope for. May we continue to do all things for His glory and may we be refined through this relationship and marriage. Please pray that we would be even a small reflection of Christ’s love for the Church, and that our marriage would be one that encourages others to want to know more about who God is!
